    Our Research Paper

    In our comprehensive study of dental disease detection, we delved into the efficacy of single-stage detection models for identifying a range of prevalent dental conditions. Our investigation was grounded in a dataset comprising a total of 1244 images, encompassing both non-Periodontitis (772 images) and Periodontitis (473 images) cases. The dataset was meticulously divided into training (70%), testing (20%), and validation (10%) sets, maintaining a balanced distribution of positive and negative cases in each subset.

    Radiology

    Radiology is a medical specialty that focuses on the use of medical imaging techniques to diagnose and treat various diseases and conditions. Radiologists use a variety of imaging techniques, such as X-rays, computed tomography (CT), magnetic resonance imaging (MRI), and ultrasound, to visualize the internal structures of the body, including the bones.

    Radiology plays an important role in the diagnosis and management of bone abnormalities, fractures, tumors, and other conditions affecting the skeletal system. For example, X-rays are commonly used to detect fractures, while CT and MRI scans are used to diagnose more complex bone injuries, such as those involving multiple fractures or soft tissue damage. Radiologists also use imaging techniques to diagnose and monitor bone-related diseases, such as osteoporosis and bone cancer. In cases where a bone biopsy is necessary, radiologists may use imaging techniques to guide the biopsy needle to the precise location of the abnormality.

    Orthopedics

    Orthopedics, also known as orthopedic surgery, is a branch of medicine that deals with the diagnosis and treatment of musculoskeletal disorders. The musculoskeletal system includes bones, joints, ligaments, tendons, muscles, and nerves, and disorders affecting any of these structures fall within the purview of orthopedics.

    Orthopedic surgeons use a wide range of diagnostic and treatment modalities to address various musculoskeletal conditions, from minor injuries to complex fractures and joint replacements. Some of the common musculoskeletal disorders that orthopedic surgeons treat include arthritis, osteoporosis, back pain, sports injuries, and congenital disorders such as scoliosis. Orthopedic treatment can range from non-surgical interventions, such as physical therapy and medication, to surgical procedures such as joint replacement, spinal fusion, and bone grafting. Orthopedic surgeons work in a variety of settings, including hospitals, clinics, and private practice, and collaborate with other healthcare professionals to ensure comprehensive care for their patients.

    Pathology

    Pathology is a medical specialty that involves the study of disease, its causes, and its effects on the body's tissues and organs. Pathologists are physicians who specialize in the diagnosis and management of disease by examining tissues, cells, and bodily fluids.

    Pathology encompasses a broad range of subspecialties, including anatomical pathology, clinical pathology, forensic pathology, and molecular pathology, among others. Anatomical pathologists specialize in the examination of tissues, organs, and bodily fluids obtained from biopsies, surgical resections, and autopsies. Clinical pathologists, on the other hand, focus on the analysis of bodily fluids, such as blood and urine, to diagnose and monitor various conditions.

    Pediatrics

    Pediatrics is a medical specialty that focuses on the care of infants, children, and adolescents. Pediatricians are physicians who specialize in the physical, emotional, and social health of children from birth through adolescence.

    Pediatricians provide a wide range of healthcare services, including preventive care, acute care for illnesses and injuries, and management of chronic health conditions. They also monitor and promote children's growth and development, and provide education and support to parents and caregivers. Pediatricians work in a variety of settings, including hospitals, clinics, and private practice. They collaborate with other healthcare professionals, such as nurses, social workers, and psychologists, to ensure comprehensive care for their patients.
